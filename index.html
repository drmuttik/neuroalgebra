Introduction

Neuroalgebra is a set of computational tools and methods that help researchers understand how brain works, be it a healthy one or not. Our approach is physical research of neuronal, astroglial, and cell-network mechanisms in the brain. (Modeling neural functions using computing techniques of industrial robotics or employing AI is outside of our interests.)

Tools

  1. ASTRO builds a simulated multi-compartmental astrocyte model that can be biophysically interrogated in the NEURON computational environment.



  2. ARACHNE is a simulation environment to build and explore complex cellular networks using the logic of NEURON.


  3. BRAINCELL is a wrapper around a popular NEURON software which extends its capabilities and adds inter-cell properties, spacial variation of parameters, external influences, stochastics, etc.


All software packages are available for free download, installation, and exploration under MIT licence.
